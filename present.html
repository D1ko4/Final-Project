Simple

The present simple is a verb tense used for actions that happen regularly or general truths.

  • I go to the gym
  • She works as a teacher
Continious

The present continuous, also known as the present progressive, is a verb tense used to describe actions happening at the current moment or around the present time

  • I am studying for my exams.
  • She is reading a book right now.
Perfect

The present perfect is a verb tense used to express actions or states that occurred in the past but have a connection to the present.

  • I have finished my homework.
  • She has traveled to many countries.
