Simple

The past simple tense is used to describe completed actions or events in the past. It is often used to talk about specific moments or a series of events that happened and concluded in the past.

  • I walked to the park yesterday.
  • She ate dinner at a restaurant last night.
Continious

The past continuous tense is used to describe ongoing actions or events in the past. It emphasizes the duration of an action that was happening at a specific time in the past.

  • They were playing football when it started raining.
  • She was studying all night for her exam.
Perfect

The past perfect tense is used to talk about an action that happened before another action or point in the past. It expresses an action that was already completed before a specific moment in the past.

  • I had already finished my homework when he called.
  • She had visited London before she moved to Paris.
